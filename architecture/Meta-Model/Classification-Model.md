# Classification Model

## Purpose
To provide a standard taxonomy that organizes Engineering Objects, enabling efficient search, automation, and tooling.

## Scope
Applies to all derived Engineering Objects.

## Taxonomies

### 1. Capability Classifications
* **Business:** Directly impacts end-user value or revenue.
* **System:** Backend processing, data management, and integrations.
* **Platform:** Infrastructure, compute, and shared services.
* **Operational:** Internal tools and business operations.

### 2. Artifact Classifications
* **Standard:** Reusable rules (e.g., coding standards).
* **Template:** Starting blocks for artifacts.
* **Specification:** Detailed technical designs.
* **Evidence:** Proof of execution or state.
* **Decision:** Recorded choices (e.g., ADRs).
* **Policy:** Non-negotiable governance rules.
* **Checklist:** Procedural verification lists.
* **Model:** Architectural representations (e.g., Meta-Model).

### 3. Review Classifications
* **Mandatory:** Required by governance for state transition.
* **Conditional:** Triggered based on specific risk profiles.
* **Advisory:** Requested for peer feedback; does not block transition.

### 4. Evidence Classifications
* **Automated:** Generated by CI/CD or tooling without human input.
* **Manual:** Human-generated proofs (e.g., sign-offs).
* **External:** Supplied by third-party auditors or systems.
* **Generated:** Synthesized from telemetry or AI.

### 5. Risk Classifications
* **Strategic:** Impacts long-term business goals.
* **Architectural:** Impacts system integrity or scalability.
* **Operational:** Impacts uptime or performance.
* **Security:** Impacts confidentiality, integrity, or availability.
