# HC-AI SDLC Constitutional Charter

## 1. Vision
To establish an engineering operating model where human creativity and accountability are seamlessly augmented by artificial intelligence, ensuring software systems are robust, traceable, and strictly governed by human intent.

## 2. Mission
To provide a deterministic, domain-driven framework that eliminates ambiguity in AI-assisted software development. The HC-AI SDLC standardizes the interactions between human engineers and AI agents, ensuring that AI acts as an accelerator for execution without assuming the authority of governance.

## 3. Core Values
- **Human Accountability**: AI assists; humans decide and remain accountable for all engineering outcomes.
- **Traceability**: Every artifact, decision, and system must have a clear lineage tracing back to its authorizing governance domain.
- **Evidence Before Acceptance**: AI-generated code, architecture, or documentation must be validated through concrete evidence (records) before being accepted into the baseline.
- **Documentation as Code**: All governance, operations, and system definitions are treated as version-controlled code.
- **Capability First**: Focus on defining the capabilities needed to solve human problems before selecting implementation technologies.

## 4. Guiding Principles
*(Note: These principles synthesize the legacy guidelines from EOS v0.x)*

### 4.1. Engineering Discipline
- Follow the Information Architecture Standard (IAS-001) for all artifact placements.
- Preserve separation of concerns between Governance, Systems, and Records.

### 4.2. Security by Design
- All system definitions and implementations must prioritize zero-trust principles and explicit permission boundaries.

### 4.3. Production Readiness
- AI must never propose "quick fixes" that compromise long-term maintainability or bypass the Verification System.

## 5. AI Usage Policy
- **Transparency**: AI assistants must explicitly state assumptions and clarify when information is derived versus explicitly provided.
- **Authority**: AI shall not modify Governance (Level 1) or Operational (Level 2) artifacts without explicit human directive and approval.
- **Scope**: AI assistants operate within the bounds defined by the Agent Operating Standard (AOS-001).
